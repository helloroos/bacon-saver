In the United States, food waste is estimated at around 30-40% of the food supply and it accounts for 20% of the total waste stream (USDA, USEPA). Our landfills are so tightly packed that they create an environment that is mostly free from oxygen, making it nearly impossible for food items to biodegrade. This is very harmful for the environment because when food items degrade without oxygen a greenhouse gas called methane is produced and while Co2 persists in the atmosphere for centuries, or even millennia, methane “warms the planet on steroids” for 20 years before decaying into CO2 (IPCC).
